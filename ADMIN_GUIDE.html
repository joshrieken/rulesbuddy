Admin Guide Prototype

For admins — the people who keep rulebooks accurate, answers trustworthy, and the community healthy. Everything here lives under /admin and the per-game review screens.

This is a prototype draft intended to become the in-app Help → For admins section. Wording and screenshots will be filled in against the live UI.

Your role

An admin can do everything a user can, plus the moderation and curation work below. Access is granted by capability, so your account simply has the admin capability — there's nothing to toggle per page.

Three things only you can do that users cannot:

  • See extracted rulebook text — the "View as HTML" page is admin-only (users only ever see source names and cited passages).
  • Approve what becomes searchable — pending rulebooks aren't used in answers until you publish them.
  • Decide what the community trusts — promoting, rejecting, and re-approving answers.

Reviewing rulebooks

When a rulebook is uploaded, it's extracted in the background and lands in pending review. It is not used in answers until you publish it. On /games/:id/review you:

  1. Open the source in the reader and skim the extracted text against the real book.
  2. Pay attention to low-confidence pages (next section) — those are where extraction was unsure.
  3. Approve to publish (it becomes retrievable) or reject if extraction failed badly.
Quality gate: only published documents are retrievable. A lower-quality upload sits in pending until you approve it, so a bad scan never silently feeds wrong answers.

Low-confidence pages

Extraction scores every page. Pages where the app's two independent reads disagreed and couldn't be reconciled get an amber "Low-confidence extraction" badge in the reader, with a per-source count of how many to check.

For each flagged page you can:

  • Fix it inline — edit the text against the source. Editing re-chunks, re-embeds, and refreshes generated metadata for the game.
  • Re-extract (stronger model) — re-render just that page and re-read it at the top tier. A background job swaps in the result when done; you don't wait.

Cleaning text

Raw PDF text is often messy — broken line wraps, headers/footers, column soup. The Clean button runs each page through an LLM to produce a tidy version. Pick a strength: Light, Standard, or Aggressive (remembered per browser).

ButtonEffect
CleanFirst-pass clean of pages that have none yet.
Clean againRe-clean the already-cleaned text for a further pass.
Wipe & cleanClear all prior cleaned text and re-clean every page from scratch, then re-chunk.
It's safe to walk away. Cleanup is a durable, resumable background job — it cleans one page at a time, writes each back as it finishes, and survives a server restart. A live counter shows progress. Cleaned text auto-applies and stays editable.

Moderating Q&A

Users ask questions and vote on answers. Your job is to keep the good ones visible and the bad ones out of circulation.

On /admin/questions and /admin/threads you can review what's being asked and answered across every game. Each answer carries a confidence level (Low → Verified) and community vote tallies to help you triage — start with high-traffic questions that have low confidence or split votes.

SignalWhat it tells you
Verified (5/5)Community-confirmed via upvotes. Usually safe.
Strong (4/5)Quotes the rulebook with a page. Trustworthy but unconfirmed.
Good / Fair (3–2)Partial citation or single-source reuse. Spot-check these.
Low (1/5)No matched passage — the model reasoned. Verify before promoting.
Split votesUsers disagree. Read it against the rulebook and decide.

Promoting to the FAQ

The FAQ page (/games/:id/faq) is the curated, community-facing Q&A for a game. From review you can promote a good answer into the canonical FAQ or reject one that shouldn't spread. Promotion makes an answer more trusted and more likely to be reused for similar future questions.

After you edit a rulebook

Important. Cached answers were written against specific rulebook text. When you add, edit, clean, or delete rulebook content, the app invalidates the answer pool for that game: auto-generated cached answers are demoted, and community answers are flagged needs_review and skipped until you re-approve them. So after a meaningful edit, expect to revisit the affected community answers and re-approve the ones that still hold.

Users & invites

  • /admin/users — see accounts and change roles. Granting the admin role gives the admin capability (all the powers in this guide).
  • /admin/invites — registration can require an invite code. Issue codes to onboard people and revoke ones you no longer want used.

Voter influence scales with reputation: contributors whose answers earn net upvotes have their votes weighted more heavily (up to a cap), so established users help surface good answers faster.

Catalog & requests

  • /admin/catalog — curate which games the catalog covers.
  • /admin/requests — users can flag a game they want supported; handle those here.

Security & system

  • /admin/security — security overview.
  • /admin/db — inspect the database directly.
  • /oban — watch background jobs (extraction, cleanup, embedding, answering). If something seems stuck, this is where you look.
Copyright reminder. Original rulebook files are never downloadable by anyone, and the extracted-text view is admin-only. Don't paste full rulebook text anywhere users can reach it — the cited passage inside an answer is the only rulebook text users are meant to see.
