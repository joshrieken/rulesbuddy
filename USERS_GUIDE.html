User Guide Prototype

Stuck on a rule mid-game? Ask Rule Maven in plain English and get an answer that quotes the exact page of the rulebook. No more flipping through the manual.

This is an early draft of the in-app Help for users. It'll get screenshots and polish later.

What is this?

Rule Maven knows the rules to your board games. Behind the scenes it has read the rulebook, so you can just ask it questions like you'd ask the friend who always knows the rules — except it shows you the page it got the answer from, so you can trust it.

Ask anything
Plain English. "Can I trade during someone else's turn?"
See the proof
Every answer quotes the rulebook and the page.
Get better over time
Good answers get reused, so popular questions are instant.

Find your game

The home page is your game library. To get to a game's question page:

  • Search by name — the list filters as you type.
  • Filter by category (board games, card games, RPGs, and more).
  • Open expansions inline if a game has them.
  • Mark games as owned (your collection) or favorite them for quick access.

Tap a game to open its question page.

Ask a question

  1. Type your question in plain English and send it.
  2. An answer appears in a moment — you'll see "Thinking…" first, then the real answer fills in. You don't have to wait on the screen.
  3. Ask a follow-up and it remembers the conversation. Your questions are grouped into threads you can switch between.
Shortcuts: use the quick-ask suggestions to start with common questions. If your game has expansions, flip the toggle to include their rules in the answer. There's also a "Did you know?" rule tip that stays pinned at the top while you play.

Reading an answer

Each answer comes with a cited passage — the actual rulebook text it used — and the page number it's on. That quote is your proof: if you want to double-check, you know exactly where to look.

If the rules don't actually cover your question, Rule Maven will say so rather than make something up. (You can reveal those "no answer" replies with Show refused.)

The confidence meter

Under each answer is a little bar showing how sure Rule Maven is. The more bars filled, the stronger the backing. Tap the ? next to it any time to see what the level means and how it could get stronger.

MeterMeans
VerifiedOther users upvoted this same answer. The community has confirmed it.
StrongQuotes the rulebook and points to the exact page. Straight from the book.
GoodQuotes the rulebook, but the exact page couldn't be pinned down.
FairReused from an earlier answer with no quote, and not confirmed by others yet.
LowNo exact passage matched — it's the app's best read of the rules. Double-check anything important.
Want a stronger answer? The ? bubble tells you the next level and how to reach it — often by tapping Regenerate to pull a fresh, cited answer, or by getting upvotes from other users.

Voting & the community

You and other users make the answers better:

  • 👍 / 👎 — tell Rule Maven whether an answer was helpful.
  • See the tally — answers show how many 👍 and 👎 they've gotten from users. When enough people agree an answer is right, it becomes Verified and is reused for everyone asking the same thing.
  • Regenerate — not happy with an answer? Get a fresh one pulled from the rulebook.

The more your upvoted answers help others, the more your votes count toward confirming answers.

Cheat sheets & extras

  • Cheat sheet — a condensed, one-glance summary of a game's rules. Great for setup and reminders.
  • Setup checklist — step-by-step setup you can tick off; it remembers what you've checked.
  • Did you know? — a random rule tip to learn something new about the game.
  • Voices — for fun, you can have an answer re-told in a different persona's style.

Add your own game

Don't see your game? You can add it:

  1. Use New Game, give it a name and category.
  2. Add the rulebook — upload a PDF, Word doc, web page, spreadsheet, photos of the pages, or just paste the text.
  3. Give it a minute to read the rulebook, then start asking.

You can also import your collection from BoardGameGeek by entering your BGG username.

About rulebooks: to respect copyright, you can't download a game's original rulebook or read its full text in the app. The only rulebook text you'll see is the quoted passage inside an answer — that's by design.

Quick answers

Why did the answer change when my friend asked the same thing?
It shouldn't drop in confidence — a reused answer keeps its rulebook citation. If you see different wording, someone may have regenerated it or the rulebook was updated.

The answer says it can't find the rule.
The rulebook may not cover it, or the wording was tricky. Try rephrasing, or tap Regenerate.

Can I trust the page numbers?
Yes — Rule Maven matches answers to the real printed page numbers, even when a book's numbering is odd.

It's taking a while.
Hard questions take a few seconds. You can leave the page; the answer will be there when you come back.
